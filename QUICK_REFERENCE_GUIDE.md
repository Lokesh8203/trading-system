## Chart Pattern Quick Reference

### Reversal Patterns (Bearish after Uptrend)
| Pattern | Signal | Stop Loss | Target |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Double Top | Break below neckline | Above second top | Neckline to top distance |
| Triple Top | Break below neckline | Above highest top | Neckline to top distance |
| Head & Shoulders | Break below neckline | Above right shoulder | Head to neckline distance |
| Rising Wedge | Break below lower trendline | Above opposite end | Wedge height |
| M Top (Bollinger) | Second top fails to reach upper band | Above first top | Pattern height |

### Reversal Patterns (Bullish after Downtrend)
| Pattern | Signal | Stop Loss | Target |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Double Bottom | Break above neckline | Below second bottom | Neckline to bottom distance |
| Triple Bottom | Break above neckline | Below lowest bottom | Neckline to bottom distance |
| Inverse H&S | Break above neckline | Below right shoulder | Head to neckline distance |
| Falling Wedge | Break above upper trendline | Below opposite end | Wedge height |
| W Bottom (Bollinger) | Second bottom fails to reach lower band | Below first bottom | Pattern height |

### Continuation Patterns
| Pattern | Direction | Entry | Stop Loss | Target |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|--------|
| Bullish Flag | Up | Break above flag | Below swing low | Pole length |
| Bearish Flag | Down | Break below flag | Above swing high | Pole length |
| Bullish Pennant | Up | Break above triangle | Below triangle low | Pole length |
| Bearish Pennant | Down | Break below triangle | Above triangle high | Pole length |
| Ascending Triangle | Up | Break above flat top | Below swing low | Triangle height |
| Descending Triangle | Down | Break below flat bottom | Above swing high | Triangle height |
| Symmetrical Triangle | Either | Breakout direction | Opposite swing | Triangle height |
| Cup & Handle | Up | Break above handle | Below handle base | Cup depth |
| Rectangle | Either | Breakout direction | Recent swing | Rectangle height |

## Indicator Quick Reference

### Moving Averages
| Type | Best Use | Settings | Signals |
|------|----------|----------|---------|
| SMA | Long-term trends | 50, 100, 200 | Golden Cross (bullish), Death Cross (bearish) |
| EMA | Short-term trends | 9, 21, 34 | Faster response to price changes |
| Trend | Support/Resistance | Price above MA = uptrend | Price below MA = downtrend |

### MACD (12, 26, 9)
| Signal | Action | Condition |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Bullish Crossover | Buy | MACD crosses above Signal (above zero preferred) |
| Bearish Crossover | Sell | MACD crosses below Signal (below zero preferred) |
| Positive Divergence | Potential reversal up | Price lower lows, MACD higher lows |
| Negative Divergence | Potential reversal down | Price higher highs, MACD lower highs |
| Histogram Expanding | Strong trend | Trade with trend |
| Histogram Contracting | Weakening trend | Prepare for reversal/consolidation |

### RSI (14)
| Level | Condition | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| 0-30 | Oversold | Look for bullish reversal patterns |
| 30-70 | Normal | Trend is healthy |
| 70-100 | Overbought | Look for bearish reversal patterns |
| Divergence | Reversal signal | Positive (price down, RSI up) = bullish; Negative (price up, RSI down) = bearish |
| 40-80 | Uptrend range | Buy near 40, avoid shorts |
| 20-60 | Downtrend range | Sell near 60, avoid longs |

### Bollinger Bands (20, 2)
| Signal | Meaning | Action |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Price at Upper Band | Resistance/Overbought | Look for reversal or continuation |
| Price at Lower Band | Support/Oversold | Look for reversal or continuation |
| Bands Expanding | Increasing volatility | Strong directional move likely |
| Bands Contracting | Decreasing volatility | Breakout/breakdown imminent |
| Squeeze | Very low volatility | Major move coming |
| M Top | Bearish reversal | Second top fails to reach upper band |
| W Bottom | Bullish reversal | Second bottom fails to reach lower band |

### Fibonacci Levels
| Level | Use | Significance |
|-------|-----|--------------|
| 23.6% | Shallow retracement | Weak pullback |
| 38.2% | Moderate retracement | Common pullback level |
| 50.0% | Mid-point | Strong psychological level |
| 61.8% | Golden Ratio | Most important level |
| 78.6% | Deep retracement | Last line of defense |
| 100% | Full retracement | Return to start |
| 161.8% | Extension target | Primary profit target |
| 200% | Extended target | Secondary profit target |
| 261.8% | Extreme extension | Aggressive profit target |

## Gap Theory Quick Reference

| Gap Type | Location | Volume | Fill Probability | Trading Action |
|----------|----------|--------|------------------|----------------|
| Common Gap | Normal trading | Normal | High (quickly filled) | Trade gap fill |
| Breakaway Gap | Start of trend, breaking pattern | High | Low (stays open) | Trade breakout direction, SL at gap edge |
| Runaway Gap | Middle of trend | Moderate | Low (measuring gap) | Trade trend continuation, gap as support/resistance |
| Exhaustion Gap | End of trend | High but weak follow-through | Moderate | Wait for reversal confirmation |

**Identification**:
- Upward Gap: Next day's low > previous day's high
- Downward Gap: Next day's high < previous day's low

## Trading Strategy Templates

### Trend Following Strategy
1. **Identify Trend**: Use 50 MA and 200 MA
   - Price above both MAs = uptrend
   - Price below both MAs = downtrend
2. **Entry**: Wait for pullback to 21 EMA or 50% retracement
3. **Confirmation**: RSI bouncing from 40 (uptrend) or 60 (downtrend)
4. **Stop Loss**: Below recent swing low (up) or above swing high (down)
5. **Target**: 2-3x stop loss distance or opposite Bollinger Band
6. **Trailing Stop**: Supertrend (10, hl2, 2.1) or Parabolic SAR

### Reversal Strategy (M Top / W Bottom)
1. **Pattern**: Identify M top (uptrend) or W bottom (downtrend)
2. **Bollinger Bands**: First extreme touches band, second fails to reach
3. **MACD Confirmation**: Histogram crossover or zero-line cross
4. **Entry**: Break of neckline or support/resistance
5. **Stop Loss**: Above first top (M) or below first bottom (W)
6. **Target**: Pattern height projected from entry
7. **Partial Profit**: 50% at 2:1 R:R, trail remainder

### Range Trading Strategy
1. **Identify Range**: Clear horizontal support and resistance
2. **Bollinger Bands**: (50, 2) to confirm range boundaries
3. **Entry Long**: Price at lower BB + ROC oversold + bullish candle
4. **Entry Short**: Price at upper BB + ROC overbought + bearish candle
5. **Stop Loss**: Beyond the band with small buffer
6. **Target**: Opposite band or mid-range for quick scalps
7. **Exit**: If range breaks, close position immediately

### Breakout Strategy with False Breakout Filter
1. **Identify Pattern**: Classical chart pattern forming
2. **Check Previous Candles**: Two candles before breakout
   - If high wicks present: Use 75-min chart for confirmation
   - If full bodies: Enter immediately
3. **Market Breadth**: Check if >60% of NSE 500 breakouts failing (if yes, skip trade)
4. **Entry**: On breakout/breakdown with confirmation
5. **Stop Loss**: 1.2 × ATR(5, RMA) from entry
6. **Target**: 2 × Stop Loss distance
7. **Profit Management**: 
   - Book 50% at target
   - Trail 50% with Supertrend (10, hl2, 2.1)
8. **Pyramiding**: If Supertrend never exits, add position after next consolidation break

## Risk Management Quick Reference

### Position Sizing
- Risk per trade: 1% of capital (conservative) to 2% (aggressive)
- Maximum open risk: 6% of capital across all positions
- Account for correlation: Don't put 2% in 3 highly correlated positions

### Stop Loss Rules
1. **Technical**: Below support (long) or above resistance (short)
2. **ATR-Based**: 1.2 × ATR(5, RMA) for volatility adjustment
3. **Pattern-Based**: Just beyond pattern boundary
4. **Percentage**: 2-3% of position value
5. **Never move stop loss away from entry** (only toward profit)

### Profit Taking
1. **Partial Profits**: Book 50% at 2:1 R:R, let 50% run
2. **Scale Out**: Take profits at multiple levels (33% at 2:1, 33% at 3:1, final 33% trailing)
3. **Trailing Stop**: Use Supertrend, Parabolic SAR, or moving average
4. **Fixed Targets**: Use classical pattern targets or Fibonacci extensions

### Risk-Reward Ratio
- Minimum: 1:2 (risk 1 to make 2)
- Preferred: 1:3 or better
- Account for: Commissions, slippage, spreads
- Never take trades below 1:1.5 R:R

## Common Trading Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Trading without stop loss** - Always define risk before entry
2. **Moving stop loss away from entry** - Accept the loss when wrong
3. **Overtrading** - Wait for high-probability setups
4. **Revenge trading** - Don't chase losses emotionally
5. **Ignoring broader market** - Context matters
6. **Position too large** - Never risk more than 2% per trade
7. **No trading plan** - Define entry, exit, stop loss before trade
8. **Fighting the trend** - Trend is your friend until it's not
9. **Ignoring volume** - Volume confirms price moves
10. **Using too many indicators** - Causes analysis paralysis

## Daily Trading Checklist

### Before Market Open
- [ ] Check global markets and overnight news
- [ ] Review economic calendar for important events
- [ ] Identify key support/resistance levels on watchlist
- [ ] Check index trend (Nifty, Bank Nifty)
- [ ] Review yesterday's unfinished trades
- [ ] Set alerts for key levels
- [ ] Plan maximum risk for the day (e.g., 3% of capital)

### During Trading
- [ ] Wait for setup confirmation before entry
- [ ] Enter position size based on risk calculation
- [ ] Set stop loss immediately after entry
- [ ] Set target orders (if using)
- [ ] Monitor position at key levels only (avoid constant watching)
- [ ] Take partial profits at predetermined levels
- [ ] Move stop to breakeven after 1:1 R:R
- [ ] Follow trading plan (don't improvise)

### After Market Close
- [ ] Review all trades taken
- [ ] Document trades in journal (entry reason, exit reason, emotions)
- [ ] Calculate P&L and win rate for the day
- [ ] Identify what worked and what didn't
- [ ] Review if rules were followed
- [ ] Plan for next trading day
- [ ] Update watchlist based on market action
- [ ] If max daily loss hit, stay away from screen

## Market Context Indicators

### Index Confirmation
- **Nifty + Bank Nifty both up**: Bullish market, safe to go long
- **Nifty + Bank Nifty both down**: Bearish market, safe to go short
- **Divergence**: One up, one down - be cautious, smaller positions

### VIX (Volatility Index)
- **VIX < 15**: Low volatility, calm market, smaller moves
- **VIX 15-25**: Normal volatility, regular trading
- **VIX > 25**: High volatility, larger stops needed, reduce position size
- **VIX > 35**: Extreme volatility, consider staying out or very small positions

### Advance-Decline Ratio
- **A/D > 2**: Strong bullish breadth, market healthy for longs
- **A/D between 0.5-2**: Neutral, mixed market
- **A/D < 0.5**: Weak market, more stocks declining, cautious on longs

## Indicator Combination Guide

### Best Combinations (Avoid Multicollinearity)

**Trend + Momentum**:
- Moving Average + RSI
- MACD + Bollinger Bands
- Moving Average + Stochastic

**Trend + Volume**:
- Moving Average + VWAP
- Supertrend + OBV
- Parabolic SAR + Volume

**Momentum + Volatility**:
- RSI + Bollinger Bands
- Stochastic + ATR
- MACD + Bollinger Bands

**Avoid These Combinations** (Redundant):
- RSI + Stochastic (both momentum)
- SMA + EMA (both moving averages)
- MACD + Moving Average Crossover (similar signals)
- Bollinger Bands + Keltner Channels (both volatility)

- **Moving Averages**: 9, 21, 34, 50, 100, 200
- **MACD**: 12, 26, 9

- **Risk per trade**: 1-2% of capital
- **Stop loss from entry**: 1.2 × ATR(5, RMA)

- **Partial profit**: Book 50% at target
- **Maximum daily loss**: 3% of capital
- **Maximum weekly loss**: 6% of capital

## Quick Decision Trees

### Should I Take This Trade?

```
Is there a clear trend or pattern? 
├─ No → Wait for better setup
└─ Yes → Continue
    │
    ├─ Do multiple indicators confirm?
    │   ├─ No → Skip or wait for confirmation
    │   └─ Yes → Continue
    │       │
    │       ├─ Is Risk-Reward at least 1:2?
    │       │   ├─ No → Skip trade
    │       │   └─ Yes → Continue
    │       │       │
    │       │       ├─ Is position size within risk limits (1-2%)?
    │       │       │   ├─ No → Reduce size
    │       │       │   └─ Yes → Continue
    │       │       │       │
    │       │       │       ├─ Do I have a clear exit plan (SL and target)?
    │       │       │       │   ├─ No → Define plan first
    │       │       │       │   └─ Yes → Take the trade
```

### Should I Hold or Exit?

```
Has price hit stop loss?
├─ Yes → Exit immediately, no questions
└─ No → Continue
    │
    ├─ Has price hit target?
    │   ├─ Yes → Take profit (full or partial based on plan)
    │   └─ No → Continue
    │       │
    │       ├─ Is the original setup still valid?
    │       │   ├─ No → Consider exiting
    │       │   └─ Yes → Continue
    │       │       │
    │       │       ├─ Are there signs of reversal (divergence, candlestick pattern)?
    │       │       │   ├─ Yes → Consider partial exit or tighten stop
    │       │       │   └─ No → Hold and trail stop
```

## Summary: Core Trading Principles

1. **Trend is Your Friend** - Trade with, not against the trend
2. **Volume Confirms Price** - High volume validates moves
3. **Confluence is Key** - Multiple confirmations = higher probability
4. **Risk Management First** - Protect capital above all else
5. **Patience Pays** - Wait for high-probability setups
6. **Plan Your Trade** - Trade your plan, define entry/exit before trade
7. **Discipline Over Emotion** - Stick to rules, ignore feelings
8. **Cut Losses Short** - Let winners run, exit losers quickly
9. **Adapt to Conditions** - Market changes, strategy must adapt
10. **Learn Continuously** - Every trade is a lesson
