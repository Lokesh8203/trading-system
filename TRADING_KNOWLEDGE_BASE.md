## Foundational Concepts

### Price Data Visualization
- **Price Over Time**: Displayed on main chart with time (x-axis) and price (y-axis)
- **Volume Subgraph**: Plotted below main chart showing shares/contracts traded
  - High volume during uptrends confirms buying interest
  - High volume during downtrends confirms selling pressure
- **Consolidation**: Price moves within a range without clear higher highs or lower lows
  - Often precedes breakouts or reversals
  - Represents market indecision

### Chart Scales
**Linear Scale**:
- Each unit on y-axis is equidistant
- Shows absolute price changes
- Suitable for short-term analysis

**Logarithmic Scale**:
- Each unit represents percentage change
- More useful for long-term charts
- Reflects proportional changes better
- Helps identify impact of moves on higher-priced stocks

### Volume Analysis
**Volume as Intensity Measure**:
- High volume with strong moves indicates high market participation
- Low volume suggests lack of conviction
- Volume spikes near tops/bottoms can signal reversals
- Exhaustion shown by sharp increase followed by drop

**Delivery Volume**:
- Number of shares/contracts actually delivered vs. traded speculatively
- High delivery volumes suggest strong conviction about direction

### Open Interest (OI)
**Definition**: Total outstanding contracts (futures/options) not yet settled
- Measures market activity but not one-sided
- Each contract involves both buyer and seller

**OI and Price Movement**:
- **Increasing OI + Price Rise**: New positions opening, trend continuation (bullish)
- **Increasing OI + Price Fall**: New positions opposite direction (bearish)
- **Decreasing OI + Price Movement**: Positions closing, potential reversal
- Short-term OI changes provide insights into reversals or continuations

## Dow Theory: Core Tenets

### Tenet I: Markets Discount Everything
- Price reflects all known information
- Exception: Unpredictable events ("acts of God")
- Trend reversal confirmed when previous high/low is broken

### Tenet II: Trends are Hierarchical
**Trend Categories**:
- **Primary Trends (Tides)**: 1+ years, major market movements
- **Secondary Trends (Waves)**: Few weeks to 3 months, corrections within primary
- **Minor Trends (Ripples)**: Up to 3 weeks, fluctuations within secondary

**For Short-Term Traders**:
- Primary: Few hours
- Secondary: 30-45 minutes
- Minor: 5-10 minutes

### Tenet III: Market Phases
1. **Accumulation Phase**: After downtrend, smart money buys, consolidation at bottom
2. **Participation Phase**: Breakout occurs, momentum picks up, broader participation
3. **Consolidation Phase**: Early buyers take profits, sideways movement
4. **Distribution Phase**: Market tops, selling pressure increases, leads to breakdown

### Tenet IV: Index Confirmation
- Multiple indexes should move in sync for valid trend
- Divergence could signal potential reversal (or false signal)

### Tenet V: Volume Confirmation
- **Uptrend**: Volume increases with price, decreases on pullbacks
- **Downtrend**: Volume increases with declines, lightens during rallies
- High volume confirms strength, low volume suggests weak corrections

### Tenet VI: Trends Persist Until Clear Reversal
- "Trend is Your Friend"
- Trend continues until definite reversal signal
- **Strategy**: Buy pullbacks in uptrend with SL, short pullbacks in downtrend with SL

## Support, Resistance, and Trendlines

### Trendlines
**Definition**: Straight line connecting 2+ price points showing trend direction

**Types**:
- **Uptrend Line**: Connects higher lows, acts as support
- **Downtrend Line**: Connects lower highs, acts as resistance

**Importance on Longer-Term Charts**:
- Stronger signals on weekly/monthly timeframes
- More touchpoints = stronger trendline
- Long-term trendlines span multiple years, higher probability of influence

### Support Levels
- Price zone where demand prevents further decline
- Typically previous low where buyers stepped in
- Acts as floor where price consolidates or rebounds

### Resistance Levels
- Price zone where selling pressure prevents rise
- Typically previous high where price stalled
- Acts as ceiling

**Longer-Term Chart Significance**:
- More reliable on longer timeframes
- Key psychological barriers
- Greater impact when interactions occur over months/years

### Support/Resistance Breaks
**Support Break**:
- Shift from bullish to bearish sentiment
- Often leads to sharp declines, stop-losses triggered
- Former support becomes new resistance

**Resistance Break**:
- Shift from bearish to bullish sentiment
- Accelerated increases, new buyers enter, shorts cover
- Broken resistance becomes new support

**Validation**:
- Repeated touches without breaks strengthen the level
- **Validated Support**: Buyers remain active, reinforces strength
- **Validated Resistance**: Sellers dominate, harder to move up

### Importance of Breaches
- Signals momentum shift
- Triggers stop-loss orders, increases volatility
- Confirms reversals
- **False breakouts/breakdowns** common - wait for confirmation

## Classical Chart Patterns

### Key Elements
1. **Risk-Reward Ratio**: Common ratio 1:3 (1 unit risk for 3 units reward)
2. **Stop Loss**: Placed below pattern's key level to limit risk
3. **Time Frame Impact**: Longer timeframes = bigger impact
4. **Volume Validation**: 
   - Decreases during consolidation (indecision)
   - Increases during breakout/breakdown (confirmation)
   - Modern markets don't always follow traditional volume rules

### Accumulation Phase
- Occurs after sustained downtrend
- Price consolidates in range, buyers/sellers in equilibrium
- Value investors accumulate despite negative news
- Price reversal signal, precedes breakout to uptrend

### Consolidation Phase
- Occurs after breakout
- Price stabilizes in narrow range
- Can lead to breakout (continuation) or breakdown (reversal)
- Volume typically declines

### Breakout Types
- **Continuation**: Breaks in direction of prior trend
- **Reversal**: Breaks opposite to prior trend

### Double Top and Double Bottom

**Double Top (Bearish Reversal)**:
- Forms after strong uptrend
- Two peaks at nearly same level
- **Neckline**: Horizontal line at lowest point between peaks
- **Breakdown**: Confirmed when price breaks below neckline
- **Stop Loss**: Just above second peak (traditional) or near neckline (improved)
- **Target**: Distance from neckline to top, projected downward

**Double Bottom (Bullish Reversal)**:
- Forms after strong downtrend
- Two troughs at nearly same level
- **Neckline**: Drawn at highest point between troughs
- **Breakout**: Confirmed when price breaks above neckline
- **Stop Loss**: Just below second trough or near neckline
- **Target**: Distance from neckline to bottom, projected upward

**Improved Risk Management**:
- Look for pullbacks to neckline after breach
- **Tranching**: Enter 1/3 on breach, 2/3 on pullback
- Tighter stop-loss near neckline improves risk-reward

**Triple Top/Bottom**: Similar structure with third peak/trough, stronger reversal signal

### Head and Shoulders

**Head and Shoulders (Bearish Reversal)**:
- Forms after uptrend
- Structure: Left shoulder, head (higher peak), right shoulder
- **Neckline**: Connects lows of left and right shoulders
- **Confirmation**: Break below neckline
- **Neckline Slope**: Downward slope = stronger bearish signal

**Inverse Head and Shoulders (Bullish Reversal)**:
- Forms after downtrend
- Structure: Left shoulder, head (lower low), right shoulder
- **Neckline**: Connects highs of left and right shoulders
- **Confirmation**: Break above neckline
- **Neckline Slope**: Upward slope = stronger bullish signal

**Stop-Loss Strategies**:
- Traditional: At nearest shoulder
- Optimized: Wait for pullback to neckline, place SL near neckline
- Tranching: Small position at breakout, add on pullback

**Modern Usage**:
- Often seen on hourly charts
- Volume confirmation helpful but not always present
- Look for patterns within patterns across timeframes

### Wedge Patterns

**Rising Wedge (Bearish)**:
- During uptrend, price converges upward
- Buying momentum slowing
- **Breakdown**: Below lower trendline signals short opportunity
- **Stop Loss**: Opposite end of wedge or recent swing high
- **Target**: Height of wedge projected from breakdown

**Falling Wedge (Bullish)**:
- During downtrend, price converges downward
- Selling pressure diminishing
- **Breakout**: Above upper trendline signals buy opportunity
- **Stop Loss**: Opposite end of wedge or recent swing low
- **Target**: Height of wedge projected from breakout

**Continuation vs. Reversal**:
- Wedge in trend direction = continuation
- Wedge against trend = reversal
- Wedge closer to recent move = likely continuation
- Wedge after long trend = likely reversal

**Duration**:
- Shorter wedges = continuation patterns
- Longer wedges = reversal signals

### Flag and Pennant Patterns

**Flag Pattern**:
- **Pole**: Strong initial move (up or down)
- **Flag**: Narrow parallelogram/rectangle sloping opposite to pole
- **Bullish Flag**: After uptrend, downward-sloping consolidation
  - Entry: Breakout above upper boundary
  - Stop Loss: Below nearest swing low
  - Target: Pole length added to breakout point
- **Bearish Flag**: After downtrend, upward-sloping consolidation
  - Entry: Breakdown below lower boundary
  - Stop Loss: Above nearest swing high
  - Target: Pole length projected downward

**Pennant Pattern**:
- Similar to flag but symmetrical triangle during consolidation
- Tighter trading ranges, more indecision
- **Bullish Pennant**: After upward move, triangle consolidation
  - Entry: Breakout above triangle
  - Stop Loss: Below lowest point in triangle
  - Target: Pole height projected upward
- **Bearish Pennant**: After downtrend, triangle consolidation
  - Entry: Breakdown below triangle
  - Stop Loss: Above highest point in pennant
  - Target: Pole length projected downward

**Timeframe**: Typically form in 1-8 weeks. Beyond 8 weeks = triangle pattern

### Triangle Patterns

**General Characteristics**:
- Consolidation patterns
- Form over 3+ months
- Trading range gradually tightens
- Target: Height of triangle (widest points), draw parallelogram to project

**Types**:

1. **Symmetrical Triangle**:
   - Lower highs and higher lows converging
   - Neutral pattern, breakout direction uncertain
   - Entry: Breakout above or breakdown below
   - Stop Loss: Near last swing low (breakout) or swing high (breakdown)

2. **Ascending Triangle**:
   - Flat top with rising lows
   - Bullish continuation pattern
   - Entry: Breakout above resistance (flat top)
   - Stop Loss: Below nearest swing low
   - Often forms after uptrend

3. **Descending Triangle**:
   - Flat bottom with lower highs
   - Bearish continuation pattern
   - Entry: Breakdown below support (flat bottom)
   - Stop Loss: Above nearest swing high
   - Often forms after downtrend

**Reliability**: More reliable after 2-3 swings within triangle

### Cup and Handle

**Characteristics**:
- Continuation pattern in uptrends
- **Cup Formation**: Correction retracing up to 2/3 of original move, rounded shape
- **Handle Formation**: Small downward-sloping consolidation near prior swing high
- **Breakout**: Above handle's resistance level
- **Confirmation**: Price surpasses previous swing high of cup
- **Stop Loss**: At base of handle (most recent swing low)
- **Target**: Depth of cup projected from breakout point
- **Volume**: Declines during cup, may increase in handle, spikes at breakout

### Rectangle Patterns

**Characteristics**:
- Consolidation between horizontal resistance and support
- Price oscillates, testing levels 3+ times
- Can last months or years
- Can be reversal or continuation pattern

**Trading**:
- **Breakout/Breakdown**: Enter when price breaches resistance/support
- **Range-Bound Trading**: Buy at support, sell at resistance while in rectangle
- **Stop Loss**: Recent swing high/low inside rectangle (not opposite end)
- **Target**: Height of rectangle projected from breakout/breakdown

### Gap Theory

**Types of Gaps**:

1. **Common/Pattern Gaps**:
   - Normal price action, filled quickly
   - Insignificant for long-term trends
   - More relevant for range-bound markets

2. **Breakaway Gaps**:
   - Start of new trend, breaking from consolidation/pattern
   - Significant volume increase
   - Often not filled in short term
   - Trade in breakout direction, SL just below/above gap

3. **Runaway Gaps**:
   - During strong trends, middle of trend
   - Signals momentum intact
   - Moderate volume increase
   - Known as "measuring gaps"
   - Gap may act as support/resistance

4. **Exhaustion Gaps**:
   - Near end of trend, potential reversal
   - High volume during gap but weak follow-through
   - Trend losing strength
   - Wait for reversal confirmation before counter-trend trades

**Gap Identification**:
- Next day's low > previous day's high = upward momentum
- Next day's high < previous day's low = downward momentum
- Analyze volume, trend context, price action follow-through
- Daily charts most reliable, also appear in intraday and weekly

**Stop Loss**: Just below (bullish) or above (bearish) gap zone, or use ATR

## Candlestick Patterns

### Key Differences from Classical Patterns
- Focus on 2-3 candlesticks vs. several candles
- Provide reversal/continuation signals
- No direct target levels defined
- Only stop-losses can be set
- Require confirmation before action

### Timeframe Reliability
1. **Daily (D1)**: Most reliable for swing/positional trades
2. **4-Hour and 1-Hour**: Good balance for short-term traders
3. **15-Min and 5-Min**: Highly prone to noise and false signals

### Single Candlestick Patterns

**Hammer (Bullish Reversal)**:
- Appears in downtrend
- Gap-down opening with strong recovery
- Small body near top of range
- No/very small upper shadow
- Long lower shadow
- Confirmation: Price moves above hammer's high within 2-3 candles
- Entry: After confirmation
- Stop Loss: Below hammer's low

**Shooting Star (Bearish Reversal)**:
- Appears in uptrend
- Gap-up opening with sell-off
- Small body near bottom of range
- No/very small lower shadow
- Long upper shadow
- Confirmation: Price moves below shooting star's low within 2-3 candles
- Entry: After confirmation
- Stop Loss: Above shooting star's high

**Inverted Hammer (Bullish Reversal)**:
- Appears in downtrend
- Price gaps down, bulls push higher, bears regain some control
- Upper shadow at least 2x body length
- Negligible/no lower shadow
- Usually green candle (more reliable)
- Confirmation: Price moves above high in next 2-3 candles
- Entry: Buy after confirmation
- Stop Loss: Below low of inverted hammer
- Less frequent than hammer but still reliable

**Hanging Man (Bearish Reversal)**:
- Appears in uptrend
- Price gaps up, bears push down, bulls recover somewhat
- Lower shadow at least 2x body length
- Negligible/no upper shadow
- Red candle more reliable (can be green)
- Confirmation: Price moves below low in next 2-3 candles
- Entry: Sell after confirmation
- Stop Loss: Above high of hanging man
- Less frequent and slightly weaker than shooting star

**Dojis**:
- Opening and closing prices same or nearly identical
- Can have long or short shadows
- Represents indecision - neither buyers nor sellers dominate
- Signals reversal, pause, or consolidation start
- Can be profit-booking signal
- Generally avoid trading solely on dojis
- "Dojis = Indecision = No Clear Direction"

**Spinning Tops and Bottoms**:
- Similar to doji but small body (not negligible)
- Relatively longer shadows
- Also signals indecision with more movement
- Both bulls and bears attempt control but fail
- Often leads to consolidation
- Reversal trades less reliable than other patterns
- Practically same as dojis from trading perspective

### Two-Candlestick Patterns

**Tweezer Bottoms (Bullish Reversal)**:
- Occurs in downtrend
- Two consecutive candles with same/nearly identical lows
- First candle: Red (bearish), continuing downtrend
- Second candle: Green (bullish), bulls gaining strength
- Big bodies and significant lower shadows = more reliable
- Confirmation: Price moves above high of larger candle
- Entry: Buy after confirmation
- Stop Loss: Just below low of tweezer bottom

**Tweezer Tops (Bearish Reversal)**:
- Occurs in uptrend
- Two consecutive candles with same/nearly identical highs
- First candle: Green (bullish), continuing uptrend
- Second candle: Red (bearish), bears gaining strength
- Big bodies and significant upper shadows = more reliable
- Confirmation: Price moves below low of larger candle
- Entry: Sell after confirmation
- Stop Loss: Just above high of tweezer top

**Bullish Engulfing (Bullish Reversal)**:
- Occurs in downtrend
- First candle: Red (bearish)
- Second candle: Green (bullish), opens below close of red, closes above body of red
- Green candle completely engulfs red candle's body
- Strong buying pressure overpowering bears
- Bigger green candle = more reliable
- Volume adds confirmation
- Confirmation: Next candle trades above high of green candle
- Entry: Buy after confirmation
- Stop Loss: Just below low of bullish engulfing candle
- Highly reliable, especially in derivative stocks with volume

**Bearish Engulfing (Bearish Reversal)**:
- Occurs in uptrend
- First candle: Green (bullish)
- Second candle: Red (bearish), opens above close of green, closes below body of green
- Red candle completely engulfs green candle's body
- Strong selling pressure overpowering bulls
- Bigger red candle = more reliable
- Volume adds confirmation
- Confirmation: Next candle trades below low of red candle
- Entry: Sell after confirmation
- Stop Loss: Just above high of bearish engulfing candle
- Creates strong momentum in derivative stocks

**Piercing Pattern (Bullish Reversal)**:
- Occurs in downtrend
- First candle: Red (bearish)
- Second candle: Green (bullish), opens below close of red, closes above midpoint (50%) of red body
- Does not engulf entire body
- Buyers regaining control but less aggressively than engulfing
- Volume on green candle adds reliability
- Confirmation: Next candle trades above high of green candle
- Entry: Buy after confirmation
- Stop Loss: Below low of previous two candles
- Weaker than engulfing pattern

**Dark Cloud Cover (Bearish Reversal)**:
- Occurs in uptrend
- First candle: Green (bullish)
- Second candle: Red (bearish), opens above close of green, closes below midpoint (50%) of green body
- Does not engulf entire body
- Sellers beginning to take control but less strongly than engulfing
- Volume on red candle adds reliability
- Confirmation: Next candle trades below low of red candle
- Entry: Sell after confirmation
- Stop Loss: Above high of previous two candles
- Weaker than engulfing pattern

## Technical Indicators

### Purpose of Indicators
Analyze price, volume, and time data to understand:
1. Trend Direction
2. Momentum
3. Volatility
4. Volume Behavior

### Combining Indicators
- Avoid multicollinearity (overlapping information)
- Use different types:
  - Trend-following (Moving Averages, Parabolic SAR)
  - Momentum (RSI, Stochastic Oscillator)
  - Volume-based (OBV, VWAP)
- Example: Pair trend indicator with momentum oscillator

### Moving Averages

**Types**:
1. **Simple Moving Average (SMA)**: Equal weight to all data points
2. **Exponential Moving Average (EMA)**: More weight to recent data, more sensitive
3. **Weighted Moving Average (WMA)**: Weighted significance to recent prices, less common

**Key Uses**:

**Trend Following**:
- Short-term MAs (5, 10, 20): Immediate trends but prone to noise
- Medium-term MAs (34, 50): Balance sensitivity and reliability
- Long-term MAs (100, 200): Long-term trend direction, major support/resistance

**Crossovers**:
- Short-term MA crossing medium/long-term MA = buy/sell opportunities
- **Golden Cross**: Short-term crosses above long-term (bullish)
- **Death Cross**: Short-term crosses below long-term (bearish)

**Recommendations**:
- Use SMA for long-term trend analysis (smooths noise)
- Use EMA for short-term trends (quick reaction)
- Use crossovers cautiously, focus on context and slopes

### Momentum Indicators

**RSI (Relative Strength Index)**:
- Measures overbought (>70) or oversold (<30) conditions
- Bounded oscillator (0-100)
- Standard period: 14 days

**William's %R**:
- Similar to RSI but inverted (-100 to 0)

**CCI (Commodity Channel Index)**:
- Measures deviation from moving average
- Unbounded oscillator
- Extreme values (+100/-100) indicate overbought/oversold
- Better for spotting extreme deviations in trending markets

### Trend-Following Indicators

**Parabolic SAR**:
- Shows potential trend reversals by plotting points above/below price
- Works well in trending markets
- Gives false signals in consolidation

**Moving Average Slope**:
- Steeper slope = stronger trends
- Flattening MAs = potential consolidation or weakening

### Volume Indicators

**VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)**:
- Combines price and volume to determine fair value

**OBV (On-Balance Volume)**:
- Tracks volume to predict price direction

### Leading vs. Lagging Indicators

**Leading Indicators** (RSI, Stochastics):
- Pros: Anticipate reversals, useful in ranging markets
- Cons: Prone to false signals in strong trends

**Lagging Indicators** (MAs, Parabolic SAR):
- Pros: Reliable in trending markets
- Cons: Late signals, less useful in ranging markets

**Recommendations**:
- Beginners: 50 and 200 SMA with RSI
- Trend Traders: EMA for fast markets, Parabolic SAR for trailing stops
- Swing/Range Traders: RSI with VWAP for overbought/oversold zones

### MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

**Components**:
1. **MACD Line**: EMA(12) - EMA(26)
   - Above zero: Uptrend
   - Below zero: Downtrend
2. **Signal Line**: 9-period EMA of MACD line
3. **Histogram**: MACD - Signal (visual representation of momentum shifts)

**Trading with MACD**:

**Crossovers**:
- MACD crosses Signal upward: Bullish signal, consider buying
- MACD crosses Signal downward: Bearish signal, consider selling
- Early entry signals allow scaling into position

**Trading in Trends**:
- Strong uptrend: MACD remains above zero
- Use crossovers above zero line to buy on dips
- Avoid shorting unless MACD enters negative zone

**Support/Resistance on MACD Line**:
- Identify levels where reversals occurred
- Anticipate potential crossovers at known levels

**MACD Divergence**:
- **Negative Divergence**: Price makes higher highs, MACD makes lower highs
  - Weakening momentum, potential reversal
- **Positive Divergence**: Price makes lower lows, MACD makes higher lows
  - Strengthening momentum, potential reversal
- Wait for divergence + MACD moving above/below zero for confirmation

**Using Histogram**:
- Crossing zero = MACD-Signal crossover
- Positive histogram: Upward momentum
- Negative histogram: Downward momentum
- Wide histogram: Strong swings, trending market
- Narrow histogram: Choppy, range-bound market

**Best Practices**:
- Works best in trending markets
- Avoid over-reliance in choppy markets
- Combine with volume analysis, support/resistance, trendlines
- More reliable in higher timeframes (daily, weekly)
- Use lower timeframes for finer entries aligned with higher timeframe trends

**Multi-Time Frame Analysis**:
- Higher timeframe (daily) to define trend
- Lower timeframe (hourly) to confirm entries
- Observe histogram reversals after peaking (trend weakening)

**Scaling**:
- Small position on initial crossovers
- Add when MACD firmly moves into desired territory

### Bollinger Bands

**Components**:
1. **Middle Band**: Simple Moving Average (typically 20-period)
2. **Upper Band**: Middle Band + 2 standard deviations
3. **Lower Band**: Middle Band - 2 standard deviations

**Key Concepts**:

**Trending Market Behavior**:
- Bull Market: Price respects upper band as resistance, middle band as support
- Bear Market: Price respects lower band as resistance, middle band as support

**Volatility Indicators**:
- Expanding bands: Increasing volatility, strong directional moves
- Contracting bands: Decreasing volatility, consolidation periods

**Sharp Moves**:
- Uptrend: Lower band angles upward (strength)
- Upper band flattens/slopes down after uptrend = correction/pause likely
- Downtrend: Upper band angles downward
- Lower band flattens/turns up = possible pause

**Patterns**:

**M or Double Top**:
- After strong upmove
- First top pierces/touches upper band
- Second top fails to reach upper band
- Indicates weakness and double-top formation
- Sell on neckline break, SL above highest point

**W or Double Bottom**:
- After strong downmove
- First bottom pierces/touches lower band
- Second bottom fails to reach lower band
- Indicates strength and double-bottom formation
- Buy on neckline break, SL below lowest point

**Squeeze Formation**:
- Bands contract sharply, low volatility period
- Often precedes breakout/breakdown
- Wait for breakout direction, enter after confirmation
- SL: Opposite side of bands

**Pinch Formation**:
- Refined version of squeeze, bands pinch tightly
- Common on smaller timeframes
- Smaller SLs, sharper moves
- Identify pinch areas, wait for sharp moves

**Timeframe-Specific**:
- Daily BB: Significant for swing/positional trades
- Hourly/15-Min BB: Key for intraday trades
- Smaller timeframes: Scalping

**Additional Insights**:
- Consistent closes outside bands = strong trend
- Overextensions often lead to mean reversion
- Price often returns to middle band after extended moves
- Combine with volume indicators (OBV, VWAP) and momentum indicators (RSI, MACD)

### RSI (Relative Strength Index)

**Formula**: RSI = 100 - [100 / (1 + (Average Gain / Average Loss))]
- Standard period: 14 days (adjustable)
- Average Gain: Sum of gains over N periods ÷ N
- Average Loss: Sum of losses over N periods ÷ N

**Levels**:
- 0-30: Oversold (potential reversal/buying opportunity)
- 30-70: Normal range (healthy trend)
- 70-100: Overbought (potential reversal/selling opportunity)

**Trading with RSI**:

**Reversal Trades**:
- **Overbought (70-100)**: Look for bearish candlestick when RSI drops below 70
  - Examples: Shooting Star, Bearish Engulfing, Doji
  - Sell signal: Bearish candlestick + RSI confirmation
- **Oversold (0-30)**: Look for bullish candlestick when RSI rises above 30
  - Examples: Hammer, Bullish Engulfing, Morning Star
  - Buy signal: Bullish candlestick + RSI confirmation

**Divergence**:
- **Positive Divergence**: Price makes lower lows, RSI makes higher lows
  - Potential reversal to upside
  - Buy signal after confirmation via bullish candlestick/pattern
- **Negative Divergence**: Price makes higher highs, RSI makes lower highs
  - Potential reversal to downside
  - Sell signal after confirmation via bearish candlestick/pattern
- Divergences can persist - wait for confirmation

**Support/Resistance in RSI**:
- RSI forms support/resistance zones during trends
- Uptrend: RSI oscillates between 40-80 (higher support)
- Downtrend: RSI oscillates between 20-60 (lower resistance)
- Buy near RSI support in uptrend
- Sell near RSI resistance in downtrend

**Advanced RSI Strategies**:

**RSI Trendlines**:
- Draw trendlines on RSI itself
- Breakout above RSI trendline in uptrend = continuation
- Breakdown below RSI trendline in downtrend = further weakness

**Dynamic Overbought/Oversold Levels**:
- Strong Uptrend: Overbought 80-100, oversold 40-60
- Strong Downtrend: Overbought 60-70, oversold 20-40

**Combining with Other Indicators**:
- Pair with Bollinger Bands, MACD, Moving Averages
- Example: Buy when RSI exits oversold AND price breaks above key MA

**Key Points**:
- RSI lags - always use with price action
- Avoid choppy markets (false signals)
- Higher timeframes more reliable for swing trading

## Fibonacci Analysis

### Fibonacci Ratios
**Key Ratios**:
- **Golden Ratio**: 1.618 or 61.8% (most significant level)
- **Retracement Levels**: 23.6%, 38.2%, 50.0%, 61.8%, 78.6%
- **Extension Levels**: 61.8%, 100%, 161.8%, 200%, 261.8%

### Types of Fibonacci Tools

**1. Fibonacci Retracement**:
- **Definition**: 2-point phenomenon measuring retracement between high and low
- **Use**: Identifies areas where pullback might reverse and resume trend
- **Application**:
  - Identify recent swing high and swing low
  - Plot from swing high to swing low (downtrend) or vice versa (uptrend)
  - Look for reversals at 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%
- **Trading**:
  - Buy on retracements in uptrend near 38.2%-61.8%
  - Sell on retracements in downtrend near same levels
  - Use candlestick patterns or indicators for confirmation

**2. Fibonacci Extension**:
- **Definition**: 3-point phenomenon projecting price movements beyond previous high/low
- **Use**: Sets profit targets in trending markets
- **Application**:
  - Identify swing high, swing low, and retracement level
  - Plot from swing low to swing high, then back to retracement
  - Key levels: 100%, 161.8%, 261.8%
- **Trading**:
  - Long trade: Set profit targets at 100%, 161.8%, 200%
  - Short trade: Use same levels on downside
  - Look for confluence with other indicators

**3. Fibonacci Projection**:
- **Definition**: 3-point phenomenon estimating potential reversal zones
- **Use**: Identifies reversal and continuation zones
- **Application**:
  - Identify three points: Swing low (A), swing high (B), retracement (C)
  - Plot projection tool, watch for action near 100%, 161.8%, 261.8%
- **Trading**:
  - Watch for reversals/breakouts at projected levels
  - Combine with patterns (double tops/bottoms, triangles, wedges)

### Trading with Fibonacci

**Combining with Price Action**:
- Use Fibonacci levels to predict bounce areas
- Validate with candlestick patterns (hammers, shooting stars, engulfing)
- Retracements to 38.2% or 50% in strong trend = continuation opportunities

**Stop Loss and Take Profit**:
- Place SL below 61.8% retracement in uptrend
- Place SL above 61.8% retracement in downtrend
- Use extension levels (100%, 161.8%, 200%) for profit targets

**Confluence Trading**:
- Look for alignment with:
  - Moving Averages (e.g., EMA 50 with 50% retracement)
  - Bollinger Bands (price reversing near band and Fibonacci level)
  - RSI Divergence (divergence at 61.8% retracement)

**Fibonacci Patterns**:
- **ABCD Pattern**: Classic pattern using retracement and projection
  - AB = CD (symmetry)
  - Combine retracement (61.8%) with projections (161.8%)
- **Gartley, Butterfly, Bat Patterns**: Complex harmonic patterns based on Fibonacci

**Squeeze and Breakout Trades**:
- Use Fibonacci retracement during consolidation to identify breakout directions

**Additional Tips**:
- Higher timeframes (daily, weekly) provide stronger levels than intraday
- Price may consolidate around Fibonacci levels (dynamic levels)
- Use levels from different swings (weekly and daily) for high-confluence zones
- Divergence + Fibonacci strengthens reversal likelihood

## Trading Strategies

### Understanding Market Environment

**Determine Market Type**:
1. **Trending**: Clear directional movement (uptrend/downtrend)
   - Use indicators: Moving averages (50 MA, 100 MA), ADX
2. **Range-Bound**: Price oscillates between support and resistance
   - Use indicators: Bollinger Bands, RSI

**Timeframe Dependence**:
- Market behavior varies by timeframe
- 1-hour may be trending while daily is range-bound
- Choose timeframe based on trading style

### Trend Following Strategies

**Strategy 1: Moving Average Crossovers**:
- Use 9 MA and 21 MA for shorter-term trend following
- **Entry Rules**:
  - Buy: 9 MA crosses 21 MA from below
  - Sell/Short: 9 MA crosses 21 MA from above
- **Exit Rules**:
  - Exit when MAs reverse crossover
  - Or use trailing stop-loss

**Strategy 2: MACD + Bollinger Bands**:
- Indicators: MACD (standard), Bollinger Bands (20, 2)
- **Rules**:
  - If Bollinger Bands slope up/down, trust MACD crossover signals
  - Ignore MACD signals in flat/horizontal Bollinger Bands
- **Stop Loss**: Below/above recent swing point or BB midline

### Contrarian/Reversal Strategies

**Setup 1: Divergences**:
- Indicators: RSI or MACD
- **Rules**:
  - Bearish divergence: Price higher high, RSI/MACD lower high
  - Bullish divergence: Price lower low, RSI/MACD higher low
- **Confirmation**: Wait for candlestick reversal or support/resistance break

**Setup 2: M Tops or W Bottoms + MACD + Bollinger Bands**:
- **M Tops**:
  - First top breaks above upper BB
  - Second top fails to break above upper BB
  - Confirm with MACD histogram crossover or zero-line cross
  - Short with SL above first top
- **W Bottoms**:
  - First bottom breaches lower BB
  - Second bottom fails to break below lower BB
  - Confirm with MACD
  - Long with SL below first bottom

### Swing Trading Strategies

**Strategy 1: Moving Averages + Bollinger Bands**:
- Indicators: 21 SMA, 34 SMA, Bollinger Bands (34, 2)
- **Uptrend**:
  - Buy near 21 SMA
  - Exit near upper BB
  - SL: Below 34 SMA
- **Downtrend**:
  - Sell near 21 SMA
  - Exit near lower BB
  - SL: Above 34 SMA
- Improvement: Use volume analysis to validate trades

**Strategy 2: 100 MA + Fibonacci**:
- Indicators: 100 MA, Fibonacci Retracement
- **Downtrend**:
  - Wait for retrace to 0.5 or 0.618 Fibonacci after one leg down
  - Short: 1/3 size at 0.5, 2/3 size at 0.618
  - SL: Above 0.786 level
  - TP: Previous leg extended to 1.618
- **Uptrend**: Reverse rules for long entries

### Range-Bound Strategies

**Setup: Bollinger Bands + ROC**:
- Indicators: Bollinger Bands (50, 2), Rate of Change (ROC)
- **Short Setup**:
  - Price near upper BB, ROC overbought
  - Wait for candlestick reversal pattern
  - Short with SL above upper band
- **Long Setup**:
  - Price near lower BB, ROC oversold
  - Confirm with reversal pattern
  - Long with SL below lower band

### False Breakout Handling

**Trading After Close**:
- Helps avoid false moves but risks missing strong rallies

**Market Breadth Method**:
- If over 60% of broader market breakouts failing, avoid trades
- Use NSE 500 watchlist to assess
- Mainly for daily trades

**Analyzing Candles**:
- Look at two candles prior to breakout
- If higher wicks present: Creates confusion, lower conviction
  - Move to 75-minute chart
  - Enter if 75-min candle closes above breakout level
- If both candles have full bodies (no major wicks): Enter immediately

**Stop Loss and Target**:
- Stop Loss: 1.2 × Previous day's ATR (5, RMA)
- Target: 2 × Stop Loss

**Partial Profit Booking**:
- Book 50% at target price
- Trail remaining 50% using Supertrend indicator (10, hl2, 2.1)

### Pyramiding

**Process**:
- If Supertrend never gave exit signal for trailing quantity, pyramid
- After breakout, market hits swing high and consolidates
- Once consolidation ends, add fresh positions (same quantity as first buy)
- Take advantage of extended move

**Key Questions to Consider**:
- Recent swing highs vs. older swing highs (near ATH)
- 2-5% gap between recent and older swing highs
- Re-entry after ATR stop-loss hit, then new breakout
- Which swing high for re-entry: recent false breakout or previous stronger highs

### Relative Strength (RS) Analysis

**Definition**: RS = Stock price / Nifty price
- Outperformer: RS increases with stock price
- Underperformer: RS decreases with stock price

**Strategy**:
- Buy outperformers, sell underperformers
- Initially used in F&O, expanded to cash markets

**StockEdge Scans**:
- **Static RS**: Measures over fixed periods (e.g., 123 days)
- **Adaptive RS**: Measures from recent lows/highs
- **RS55 Model**: 55-day RS for smaller time horizon

**Research Insight**:
- Based on Jagadish and Titman's paper
- Stocks outperforming over 1 year and 3 months likely continue
- Modified for Indian markets: 1-week and 3-month periods

### General Enhancements for All Strategies

**1. Risk Management**:
- Define Risk-Reward ratio (1:2 or 1:3)
- Limit risk per trade (e.g., 1% of capital)

**2. Confluence Trading**:
- Combine multiple indicators for stronger signals
- Example: Fibonacci + Bollinger Bands + MACD

**3. Trade Management**:
- Use trailing stop-loss to lock profits during trends
- Scale out of positions by taking partial profits at key levels

**4. Timeframe Synchronization**:
- Trade on lower timeframe (15-minute)
- Confirm trend on higher timeframe (1-hour or daily)

## Risk Management

### Position Sizing
- Risk 1% of capital per trade (conservative)
- Never risk more than 2-3% on single trade
- Account for correlation between positions

### Stop Loss Placement

**Methods**:
1. **Technical Levels**: Below support (long) or above resistance (short)
2. **ATR-Based**: 1.2 × ATR(5, RMA) for volatility adjustment
3. **Pattern-Based**: Based on classical pattern boundaries
4. **Fixed Percentage**: 2-3% of position value

### Profit Taking

**Partial Profit Booking**:
- Book 50% at initial target (2:1 or 3:1 R:R)
- Trail remaining with Supertrend or key levels
- Lock in gains while allowing for extended moves

**Trailing Stop Loss**:
- Use technical indicators (Supertrend, Parabolic SAR)
- Follow support/resistance levels
- Adjust based on volatility

### Risk-Reward Ratio
- Minimum 1:2 (risk 1 to gain 2)
- Prefer 1:3 or better
- Account for transaction costs and slippage

### Tranching/Scaling
- Enter 1/3 position at initial signal
- Add 1/3 on confirmation
- Add final 1/3 on trend continuation
- Allows for better average entry price
- Manages risk of false signals

## Advanced Techniques

### Volume Analysis
- High volume confirms trend strength
- Low volume suggests weak moves
- Volume spikes at key levels signal reversals
- Delivery percentage indicates conviction

### Multiple Timeframe Analysis
- Use higher timeframe for trend direction
- Lower timeframe for precise entries/exits
- Align signals across timeframes for confidence
- Example: Daily for trend, hourly for entry

### Indicator Confluence
- Combine different indicator types:
  - Trend: Moving Averages
  - Momentum: RSI or MACD
  - Volume: VWAP or OBV
  - Volatility: Bollinger Bands or ATR
- More confluence = higher probability setups

### Support/Resistance Clustering
- Identify levels where multiple factors align:
  - Previous swing highs/lows
  - Fibonacci levels
  - Moving averages
  - Round numbers
  - Bollinger Band levels
- High confluence zones more significant

### Divergence Trading
- Price and indicator moving opposite directions
- Signals weakening momentum
- Works with RSI, MACD, histogram
- Wait for confirmation before entry
- More reliable on higher timeframes

### Market Context
- Understand broader market conditions
- Index behavior and correlation
- Sector rotation and trends
- News and events impact
- Overall market sentiment

### Backtesting and Journaling
- Test strategies on historical data
- Document all trades with entry/exit rationale
- Review performance regularly
- Identify patterns in wins/losses
- Refine approach based on results

### Adaptability
- Market conditions change
- Strategy effectiveness varies
- Be flexible with approach
- Don't force trades in unfavorable conditions
- Preserve capital in uncertain times
